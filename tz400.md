# SonicWall TZ400 & ARRIS SB8200 Network Architecture

## The Analogy: The Shipping Dock and the Customs Checkpoint
Think of your **ARRIS SB8200** as a massive industrial shipping dock. It brings in raw cargo (internet traffic) at extremely high speeds but doesn't check what's inside the boxes. It simply drops the cargo off at the perimeter.

Your **SonicWall TZ400** is the heavily fortified Customs and Border Protection facility. Instead of letting that raw traffic flow directly into your private warehouse, the TZ400 opens the boxes (Deep Packet Inspection), checks the manifests against known threat lists (Geo-IP and Botnet filtering), and tightly controls where the safe cargo is allowed to go.

## Wiring Diagram

To maximize security, you should utilize physical port isolation on the firewall rather than plugging everything into a single switch.

1. **Coaxial Connection:** Wall outlet to ARRIS SB8200.
2. **External WAN (The Checkpoint Entrance):** ARRIS SB8200 (Port 1) to SonicWall TZ400 (**Port X1** - Default WAN).
3. **Internal LAN (The Safe Zone):** SonicWall TZ400 (**Port X0** - Default LAN) to your primary network switch. This is for your trusted devices, daily driver workstations, and personal WiFi.
4. **DMZ / Home Lab (The Quarantine Zone):** SonicWall TZ400 (**Port X2**) to a secondary switch or directly to a dedicated NIC on your server. This physically isolates externally accessible services.
