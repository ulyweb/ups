Network Architecture: SonicWall TZ400 & ARRIS SB8200

The Analogy: Shipping Dock & Customs Checkpoint

Think of your ARRIS SB8200 as an industrial shipping dock. It brings in massive amounts of cargo (internet traffic up to 2Gbps) but performs zero inspections.

The SonicWall TZ400 is your fortified Customs and Border Protection checkpoint. Instead of letting raw traffic flow directly into your private space, the TZ400 performs Deep Packet Inspection, checks origins against known threat lists, and strictly routes traffic into isolated, secure zones.

Wiring Architecture

  • WAN Link: Coax from wall → ARRIS SB8200 → Ethernet to X1 on TZ400.
  • LAN Link: TZ400 X0 → Core Switch (Trusted workstations, phones, WiFi).
  • DMZ Link: TZ400 X2 → Dedicated Home Lab Network (Proxmox, VMs).

Strategic Configuration

  1. Zone Segmentation: Keep personal devices on the highly trusted LAN zone (X0). Put externally facing assets, like a Nextcloud AIO VM, into the DMZ zone (X2).
  2. Zero-Trust DMZ Rules: Create firewall access rules that completely block the DMZ from initiating connections back into the LAN zone. This prevents lateral movement.
  3. Pre-emptive Drops: Enable Geo-IP filtering and Intrusion Prevention to drop traffic from high-risk regions before it hits your internal reverse proxies.
